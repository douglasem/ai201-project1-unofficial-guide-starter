Who We Are
Welcome to Florida Fresh Dining, where hospitality begins at the heart of the University of Florida! With over 45 dining locations, we pride ourselves on blending tradition with innovation, ensuring every Gator finds a culinary experience that resonates with their tastes and preferences. Our commitment to diversity and convenience drives us to constantly evolve, staying attuned to the latest food trends and culinary delights.

At Florida Fresh Dining, we aspire to be more than just a dining service – we're your partners in nourishment throughout your journey at the University of Florida. Whether you're a student, faculty, staff, or visitor, we're dedicated to serving you with excellence and care, enriching your campus experience one meal at a time.

Explore our array of programs and events designed to enhance your dining adventure and foster a sense of community. Join us in savoring the flavors of campus life and discover the vibrant culinary landscape that awaits you at Florida Fresh Dining.


Nutrition
Florida Fresh Dining aims to provide our students and guests with the support they deserve by accommodating those with food allergies and intolerances and providing a safe place to eat. From following standardized recipes to avoiding cross-contact in all facets of production and service, we do our best to provide safe and balanced options for those with dietary food allergies and intolerances. Be aware that we handle and prepare eggs, milk, wheat, shellfish, fish, soy, peanuts, sesame, tree nuts, and other potential allergens in the food production areas in all of our facilities. We work to provide nutritional and ingredient information that is as complete and up-to-date as possible. To view, please visit the Florida Fresh Dining website at www.floridafreshdining.com and download our Dine on Campus app for convenience. Products may change without our knowledge and menu items are prepared in close proximity to other ingredients that may result in cross-contact with ingredients not listed, including allergens. Guests with food allergies or specific dietary concerns should speak with the onsite Registered Dietitian, Sierra James, for individualized assistance. Sierra offers complimentary consultations to address any inquiries, worries, or difficulties regarding allergens or other dining matters with students.

Sustainability
Our Sustainability Initiatives at Florida Fresh Dining cater directly to students. We prioritize cage-free eggs, antibiotic-free chicken, waste reduction, and sourcing from sustainable seafood suppliers like the Monterey Bay Aquarium Seafood Watch. The Food Hall at Gator Corner, one of UF's 91 Gold LEED Certified buildings, exemplifies our commitment to sustainability recognized worldwide. We also run a program that brings local products to the Markets on campus, allowing students to use their Flex to support local producers. To keep you informed and engaged, we regularly host fun and educational DIY events.

We are proud of the Ozzi Box program that is offered in our dining halls. These boxes are a simple yet effective way to reduce single-use plastics and minimize our carbon footprint. It also gives students an easy and reliable way to take meals to-go from the dining halls. Florida Fresh Dining is committed to sustainability, and with every Ozzi box purchased, we'll donate $5 to Field and Fork, UF's on-campus food pantry. Join us in making a difference for a greener future!

Joy-Ful
Throughout the school year, students will be invited to participate in four signature Joy-Ful events, simultaneously taking place across campuses nationwide. The first signature event kicks off in September with a welcome back "Food Festi-Ful," featuring a locally-inspired menu and unique celebrations. Moving into November, we'll host Thank-Ful around Thanksgiving, offering classic Thanksgiving dishes and activities to show gratitude on campus.

Come February, around Valentine's Day, students can anticipate attending DelighFul, which coincides with Mardi Gras and features a New Orleans-inspired menu. We aim to inspire over 500,000 acts of kindness nationwide during this festive celebration. As we approach the end of the spring semester in April, we'll celebrate JoyFul themed around ThoughtFul, treating students to a wellness and self-care-centered event featuring climate-friendly dishes and Feel Good Foods.

All these events occur simultaneously, fostering a sense of community and togetherness among students across the country.

Feedback 
At Florida Fresh Dining, we're committed to continuously improving our program based on your feedback. Your input helps us maintain quality and refine our offerings to better serve the UF community.

In our dining halls, we provide convenient ways for you to share your thoughts. You'll find Happy or Not stands where you can quickly rate your overall experience as you leave. Additionally, we offer dedicated text lines for each dining hall, as well as a general text line for meal plans or any dining-related questions or concerns. If you prefer to provide feedback online, you can use the feedback form on our website here.

But we don't stop there. In October, we conduct our annual Voice to Vision survey. This brief survey takes about 10 minutes to complete and provides invaluable insights that guide our menu creation, the development of new dining concepts, and the planning of impactful events.

Come Spring, we host On-Site Insights, intimate focus groups where students have the opportunity to share detailed feedback in a safe and constructive environment. These sessions, typically lasting an hour, allow us to delve deeper into your suggestions and ideas.

Your feedback doesn't just end with surveys and focus groups. It inspires our "You Spoke, We Listened" initiative, where we implement improvements and adjustments based directly on your input. At Florida Fresh Dining, your voice truly makes a difference, shaping the dining experience for everyone in our community.
